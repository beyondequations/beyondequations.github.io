Modeling Gravitational Interactions

Published: March 02, 2025 | Category: Physics

Newton’s law of gravitation describes the force between two masses: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \), where \( G \) is the gravitational constant, \( m_1 \) and \( m_2 \) are masses, and \( r \) is the distance. I’ve been simulating solar system orbits using this equation.

\( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)

The results show stable elliptical paths—pretty cool for a weekend project!
